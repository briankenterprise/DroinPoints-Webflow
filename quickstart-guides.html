Carrying Case Prep

Our target system helps make things easy in the field. Here’s a short list of steps to make sure you fumble around less when it’s time to place your ground points.

1

Before you go

Setting Up the Carrying Case

Before engaging the ready case, open box along the taped seam and inspect contents. If you purchased a kit with 60D nails you can remove them at this time.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

2

Before you go

Open the Access Panel

Punch Through the Tab: Carefully press your finger against the “Open Here” tab to punch it through. This action will create an initial opening to facilitate the next steps.

Cut or Tear Along the Perforated Line: Using this initial opening, either cut with a knife or tear along the perforated dotted line extending in both directions from the tab. Ensure that you are following the line precisely to create a neat opening.

Fold Along the Solid Line: Once the perforated line has been cut or torn, fold the material along the adjacent solid line to fully open the access panel.

Warning: If you're using a knife to cut along the perforated line, be extra cautious to avoid personal injury. Additionally, be mindful of the pressure and depth of your cut to prevent any damage to the targets within the box.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

3

Before you go

Loading the Nails

8" nails can be inserted into the slots but targets may be difficult to remove due to overcrowding.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

4

Before you go

Use the Handle

Punch through the handle to complete the carrying case setup. Find the designated handle area on the top of your carrying case. Press down firmly along the perforated edges to punch through, then fold along the solid line to form a durable handle. This feature enhances the portability of your DronePoints carrying case, making it even more convenient for fieldwork.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

5

Before you go

Remove Targets from the Carrying Case

Place the carrying case face down with the access panel up. Gently slide your fingers under the top target and lift it straight upwards. A slight twist can help de-nest and ease the target out. This method ensures you can quickly and smoothly begin your field setup without any trouble.

How to Place Targets on Unpaved Surfaces

Setting up DronePoints Aerial Targets is quick and straightforward—just secure each target with a single nail for reliable placement on unpaved ground surfaces. We offer target kits with included ground fastening nails to make the process as quick and easy as possible, all you need is a hammer.

Important Note: No problem if you want to use your own nails but please be advised 60D common nails should always be used with our specialty adapters which provide a more secure hold and improve accuracy.

1

Unpaved Surfaces

Prep the ground surface

Start by positioning the target at your desired location, ensure the following:

  1. The area is flat and clear of vegetation or debris.
  2. Maintain a clear view of the sky for the target, free from obstructions like trees or buildings
Next
arrow_forward_ios

2

Unpaved Surfaces

Nail Target to Ground Surface

Start by positioning the target at your desired location, ensure the following:

  1. Using a 60D common nail (recommended)- Our specially designed 60D adapters are required when using this nail type to ensure a firm and lasting hold, as well as delivering increased accuracy.
  2. Using 80D common nail - Can be used without adapters.
Next
arrow_forward_ios

3

Unpaved Surfaces

Mark Center

Enhance center visibility and deter unauthorized removal.

  1. Remove the marker from its packaging and align with the centering perforations on the target center, adhesive side down.
  2. Press firmly to adhere the marker to the target.
Next
arrow_forward_ios

4

Unpaved Surfaces

Survey

To ensure precise GPS observations we recommend using a high accuracy GPS/GNSS system

  1. Gently place and rest the survey rod through the center marker hole and on top of the head of the affixed nail.
  2. Obtain the coordinate.

How to Place Targets paved Surfaces

This is a short description or overview of placing targets on paved surfaces. This can be about 2 sentences long and better to keep it short and sweet!

1

paved Surfaces

Prepare the Stencil

Cut the flaps

  1. Identify Perforated Lines: Begin by locating the perforated lines situated at the bottom edges of the indented regions on the target. These lines mark where to cut to transform your stencil into a functional stencil.

For Low Altitude
‍This option provides a marking with crosshairs that are 1.25 inches wide. Perfect for data collections with a high ground sample distance (GSD) aerial data collections performed at lower flight altitudes.

For High Altitude
This option provides a marking with crosshairs that are 2.0 inches wide. This option is for lower ground sample distance (GSD) aerial data collections performed at higher flight altitudes.

  1. Cut with Care: With a sharp knife or a pair of scissors, carefully cut along these perforated lines. Ensure each cut is clean and follows the line closely to maintain the integrity of the stencil design.

paved Surfaces

Flip the stencil

For High Altitude ONLY
Flip the Stencil Over: The underside of the target must be facing upwards.

2

paved Surfaces

Spray Center

Position the Stencil: Carefully place your DronePoints stencil at the intended location. If you're aligning it over an existing survey monument or nail, utilize the center hole of the stencil for precise positioning.

Spray the Center: With the stencil stabilized, apply your marking paint through the center hole. This mark not only guides you for any necessary realignment but also serves as a critical reference point for GPS surveys.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

3

paved Surfaces

Fold Flaps Up

Prepare for Paint Free Handling: Before applying any paint, gently fold all the cut flaps upwards and towards the center of the stencil. This creates makeshift handles, allowing you to manipulate the stencil without getting paint on your fingers.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

4

paved Surfaces

Spray Crosshairs

Spray Each Crosshair Region: Carefully apply spray paint to each interior region designated for the crosshairs. Aim for uniform and adequate paint coverage across all areas to enhance visibility and accuracy of the markings.

Tip for Optimal Application: Keep the stencil flush against the ground while spraying. If you notice any part of the stencil lifting, gently apply pressure towards that direction using the handles. This helps maintain contact with the surface, preventing under spray or blurry edges.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

5

paved Surfaces

Spray Around Edges

Apply Paint to Outer Edges: Holding the spray paint can steady, begin to apply paint around the perimeter of the stencil. Aim for a consistent band of paint that extends at least 2 inches beyond the stencil’s edge. A thicker boundary is preferable for ensuring high visibility.

Next
arrow_forward_ios

6

paved Surfaces

Lift Up

Straight Upward Motion: Gently lift the stencil directly upwards with a steady, even motion. It's crucial to avoid any sideways movement to prevent smearing the freshly applied paint.
